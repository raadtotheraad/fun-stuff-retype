General Overview
Scientific Name: Citrus limon
Family: Rutaceae
Origin: Lemons are thought to have originated in the Assam region of India, northern Myanmar, or China.
Type: Citrus fruit
Appearance: Bright yellow when ripe; oval shape with a pronounced nipple at one end and smooth or slightly textured peel.

Health Benefits
Rich in Vitamin C:
Boosts immune function.
Helps in collagen production for skin and tissue repair.
Antioxidant Properties:
Neutralizes free radicals, reducing cell damage.
Aids Digestion:
Lemon water can relieve bloating and indigestion.
Supports Heart Health:
Contains flavonoids that may reduce the risk of heart disease.
Weight Management:
Low-calorie and aids in hydration, often used in detox regimens.
Prevents Kidney Stones:
Citric acid increases urine pH, reducing the formation of stones.
Uses of Lemons
Culinary:
Juices, desserts, marinades, dressings, and zest in baking.
Beverages:
Lemonade, flavored water, teas, and cocktails.
Medicinal:
Sore throat remedies (honey and lemon mixtures).
Cleaning:
Lemon juice is a natural degreaser and disinfectant.
Cosmetic:
Used in DIY skincare for brightening and exfoliation.
Varieties of Lemons
Eureka:
Most common in stores, thick-skinned and juicy.
Lisbon:
Similar to Eureka but with fewer seeds.
Meyer Lemon:
Sweeter and less acidic, with a thinner skin.
Ponderosa:
Large and thick-skinned, often used as ornamental plants.
Fun Facts
Lemons were highly valued during the 18th century as a scurvy preventative for sailors.
The phrase "When life gives you lemons, make lemonade" is a popular metaphor for turning challenges into opportunities.
Lemons can power a small LED light through their acidic properties when used in a simple battery setup.
